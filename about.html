Our Story

The journey from farm to table

Our Beginning

Time For Thyme was born from a simple yet profound idea: to create a restaurant that honors the connection between the earth, the farmer, and the diner. Founded in 2015 by Chef Emily Chen and sustainable agriculture advocate Michael Thompson, our restaurant began as a small farm-to-table concept with just 12 tables.

Chef Emily, with her background in classic French cuisine and experience in Michelin-starred restaurants across Europe, wanted to bring sophisticated cooking techniques to locally sourced ingredients. Michael, having spent years working with local farmers to promote sustainable practices, provided the connection to the agricultural community that would become the foundation of our restaurant.

Together, they transformed an old brick building in Charlotte into a warm, inviting space where guests could experience the true flavors of locally grown, seasonal ingredients prepared with care and creativity.

Our Philosophy

Local Sourcing

We believe in the power of local. By sourcing ingredients from farms within a 100-mile radius, we reduce our carbon footprint, support local economies, and ensure the freshest possible ingredients for our dishes.

Culinary Excellence

We blend traditional techniques with modern innovations to create dishes that are both familiar and surprising. Our goal is to highlight the natural flavors of our ingredients through thoughtful preparation.

Sustainability

Every decision we make, from our menu design to our waste management practices, is guided by our commitment to environmental sustainability and responsible stewardship of resources.

Our Journey

2015

Grand Opening

Time For Thyme opens its doors with a small but dedicated team and a commitment to local, sustainable cuisine.

2016

First Recognition

Named "Best New Restaurant" by Charlotte Magazine and received first critical acclaim for our seasonal menus.

2018

Rooftop Garden

Established our rooftop herb garden, allowing our chefs to harvest fresh herbs just steps from the kitchen.

2020

Community Support

During challenging times, we pivoted to provide meals for healthcare workers and vulnerable community members.

2022

Expansion

Opened our private dining space and expanded our main dining room to accommodate growing demand.
