Our Sustainability Commitment

How we're making a difference, one meal at a time

At Time For Thyme, sustainability isn't just a trending concept—it's at the heart of everything we do. From the food we serve to the energy we use, we're committed to making choices that respect our environment and support our community.

Locally Sourced Ingredients

We partner with local farmers and producers within a 50-mile radius whenever possible. This reduces transportation emissions, supports the local economy, and ensures the freshest ingredients for our dishes.

Our team visits each farm personally to verify sustainable growing practices and ethical treatment of workers and animals.

Zero Waste Initiative

We're proud to be working toward a zero-waste kitchen. We compost all food scraps, recycle diligently, and have eliminated single-use plastics from our operations.

Our chefs are trained in nose-to-tail and root-to-stem cooking methods to minimize waste and maximize flavor. Vegetable trimmings become delicious stocks, stale bread transforms into croutons, and citrus peels are preserved for cocktail garnishes.

Rooftop Garden

Our rooftop garden supplies many of the herbs and certain vegetables used in our kitchen. This urban growing space reduces our carbon footprint while providing the freshest possible ingredients.

The garden also serves as a rainwater collection site, helping us reduce water usage in our operations. We use sustainable growing practices and avoid harmful pesticides and chemicals.

Energy Efficiency

Our restaurant runs on 100% renewable energy through solar panels and wind energy credits. We've invested in energy-efficient appliances, LED lighting, and smart thermostats to reduce our consumption.

Our building has been retrofitted with improved insulation and energy-efficient windows to minimize heating and cooling requirements.

Community Engagement

We believe sustainability extends beyond environmental practices to include social responsibility. That's why we offer regular cooking classes focused on plant-based eating and sustainable food practices.

We also partner with local food banks to donate excess food and participate in community gardens that provide fresh produce to underserved neighborhoods.
